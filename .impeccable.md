# Impeccable Design Context — AISPEG

This file provides project-specific design context for the impeccable skill family.
It is read before any design work and keeps future design decisions coherent.

## Design Context

### Users

Three overlapping audiences, prioritized in this order:

1. **Institutional stakeholders** — the President, Provost, Deans, ORED leadership,
   working-group members (Operational Excellence, Strategic Enrollment,
   Faculty Development, Internal Audit). They view the site on desktop browsers
   during business hours, often alongside other institutional tools. They are
   AI-skeptical by default and well-calibrated against hype. The job-to-be-done
   is *"help me quickly see what AI work is happening at UI, who owns each
   piece, and what it means for my unit"*.

2. **AISPEG and partner-unit practitioners** — IIDS staff, partner-unit
   co-builders (SEM, UCM), AI4RA partners at SUU. They need operational detail:
   schemas, relationships, governance artifacts, the ingestion portal. They
   trust the tool and want density.

3. **Peer institutions** — higher-ed AI/research-admin professionals reached
   through REACH, NSF GRANTED, and AI4RA. They arrive through a public link
   and are evaluating whether they can learn from or adopt what UI is doing.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **institutional · evidence-forward · quietly-confident.**

- The register of a well-made academic research center: precise, grounded,
  a bit dry but not stuffy. Closer to a *Nature* article than a *TechCrunch*
  post.
- Emotional goal: reassurance for stakeholders, legitimacy for peers, respect
  for practitioners' time. Not delight. Not excitement. *Trust.*
- Evidence-forward means: every claim should be traceable to a named owner,
  a home unit, a visibility tier, or a documented artifact. The site itself
  demonstrates the operational-excellence principle that AISPEG advocates.

### Aesthetic Direction — Match Institutional Brand

**The site is a University of Idaho institutional property. It should look
and feel like one.** Match the live uidaho.edu visual language — don't
invent a parallel aesthetic.

**Colors (canonical — do not substitute):**

- **Pride Gold** `#F1B300` — the primary accent. Vivid yellow-gold. Not a
  muted khaki; not a metallic. Use for emphasis, active CTAs, focus outlines,
  highlight fills behind key words.
- **Brand Black** `#191919` — ink and dark surfaces. Not pure black.
- **Brand White** `#FFFFFF` — primary surface.
- **Silver** `#808080` — secondary neutral.
- **Huckleberry** `#261882` — deep purple, secondary brand.
- **Lupine** `#5E48FF` — vivid violet, secondary brand.
- **Clearwater** `#008080` — teal. Used on uidaho.edu for link underlines.

**Typography (single-family system, matches uidaho.edu):**

- **Public Sans** as the single type family, variable weight 100–900
  (loaded via `next/font/google`).
- Headings use **weight 900** for strong display contrast — this is the
  institutional pattern on uidaho.edu.
- Body uses weight 400; emphasis uses weight 600–700.
- Italic available via the italic axis; used sparingly for emphasis and
  owner-name treatments.
- No display serif pairing. The live UI brand is sans-only. Typographic
  variety comes from **weight contrast**, not font contrast.

**Site surface system:**

- Light mode. Near-white backgrounds; brand-black ink.
- Pride Gold is rare — used for active focus, the emphasis highlight behind
  one or two words on a page, the primary CTA.
- Links: underline in clearwater teal (`#008080`) at 1.5px, 4px offset,
  matching uidaho.edu's treatment.
- Secondary colors (Huckleberry, Lupine) used for **section accents and
  status chips** where institutional variety is helpful — not wallpaper.

**Deck (reveal.js) surface system:**

- Dark — background is brand-black `#191919`.
- Display typography in Public Sans weight 900 with tight tracking.
- Gold `#F1B300` as the single dominant accent; small use of Clearwater and
  Lupine for variety across the deck's 16 slides (never on the same slide).
- Owner names rendered with italic + gold to match the site's emphasis
  convention.

**References (inspiration, not imitation):**

- **uidaho.edu** itself — the authoritative visual reference.
- `ia.net` — for typography restraint and editorial confidence in the
  single-family sans approach.
- USWDS (Public Sans' home system) — for institutional-register sans usage.

**Anti-references (explicit don'ts):**

- My own previous site chrome — drab khaki `#B5A36A` accents, Literata
  serif, left-border callouts, uniform card grids. All wrong for the UI
  brand.
- Generic SaaS aesthetics (Linear/Vercel template look).
- Dark-gradient + neon accent "AI slop."
- Mixing serif display with sans body. The UI brand is sans-only.

### Accessibility

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 AA for all public pages.
- **Reduced motion:** respect `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere,
  especially in the deck and hover reveals.
- **Projector visibility:** deck body text ≥ 28pt equivalent. Use Public
  Sans 600+ for projector legibility. Avoid thin strokes.
- **Color-blind:** status chips carry a label, not color alone (already
  true in the Intervention schema). Pride Gold on white has good contrast
  (check AA), but for small text pair gold with brand-black ink rather than
  as text color on white.

## Design Principles

1. **Every claim names a human.** The page that shows what UI is doing
   must show *who is doing it*. Owner names are load-bearing UI.
2. **Restraint > decoration.** Pride Gold is rare. No gold stripe on every
   card, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no bouncy motion. If a visual
   element doesn't carry information or weight, cut it.
3. **Density with hierarchy — achieved through weight, not font pairing.**
   Public Sans 900 headings against 400 body create the hierarchy.
   Stakeholders scan, practitioners read detail. A ≥1.25× type scale
   between steps.
4. **Brand fidelity over invention.** If the live uidaho.edu treats a UI
   element a certain way (link underline teal, heading weight 900), match
   it. Do not invent a parallel institutional aesthetic.
5. **The site demonstrates what it argues.** AISPEG preaches
   evidence-forward, owner-named, compliance-aware AI work. The site
   chrome must live up to that — every design choice should be defensible
   as functional.
