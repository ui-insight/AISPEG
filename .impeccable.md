# Impeccable Design Context — AISPEG

This file provides project-specific design context for the impeccable skill family.
It is read before any design work and keeps future design decisions coherent.

## Design Context

### Users

Three overlapping audiences, prioritized in this order:

1. **Institutional stakeholders** — the President, Provost, Deans, ORED leadership,
   working-group members (Operational Excellence, Strategic Enrollment,
   Faculty Development, Internal Audit). They view the site on desktop browsers
   during business hours, often alongside other institutional tools. They are
   AI-skeptical by default and well-calibrated against hype. The job-to-be-done
   is *"help me quickly see what AI work is happening at UI, who owns each
   piece, and what it means for my unit"*.

2. **AISPEG and partner-unit practitioners** — IIDS staff, partner-unit
   co-builders (SEM, UCM), AI4RA partners at SUU. They need operational detail:
   schemas, relationships, governance artifacts, the ingestion portal. They
   trust the tool and want density.

3. **Peer institutions** — higher-ed AI/research-admin professionals reached
   through REACH, NSF GRANTED, and AI4RA. They arrive through a public link
   and are evaluating whether they can learn from or adopt what UI is doing.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **institutional · evidence-forward · quietly-confident.**

- The register of a well-made academic research center: precise, grounded,
  a bit dry but not stuffy. Closer to a *Nature* article than a *TechCrunch*
  post.
- Emotional goal: reassurance for stakeholders, legitimacy for peers, respect
  for practitioners' time. Not delight. Not excitement. *Trust.*
- Evidence-forward means: every claim should be traceable to a named owner,
  a home unit, a visibility tier, or a documented artifact. The site itself
  demonstrates the operational-excellence principle that AISPEG advocates.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Site (public pages):** Light. Editorial-academic. Information-dense without
feeling cramped. Confident left-aligned typography. Restraint in color —
the U of I Gold (`#B5A36A`) is a rare accent, not wallpaper.

**Deck (reveal.js presentations):** Dark. Stage-optimized for conference
rooms and projectors. Same typographic system scaled for viewing distance.

**References (inspiration, not imitation):**
- `ia.net` — for typography and editorial restraint
- `observable.com` — for information-dense, low-chrome, confident layouts
- Early Knight Lab at Northwestern (academic research-lab project showcase)

**Anti-references (explicit don'ts):**
- Generic SaaS landing aesthetic (`linear.app`, `vercel.com` template feel)
- Dark gradient + neon accent "AI slop"
- The site's own prior chrome: left-border accent stripes on callouts,
  uniform card grids, gold bar on every section header

**Color system:**
- Primary palette anchored on U of I institutional colors:
  `ui-gold #B5A36A` (rare accent), `ui-charcoal #1a1a2e` (ink), warm off-white surface
- Tint neutrals toward gold hue subtly (chroma ~0.005–0.01) to create
  subconscious cohesion
- Use OKLCH for new palette work; reduce chroma at high lightness
- Reserve accent gold for emphasis moments — names of operational owners,
  status chips that demand attention, primary CTAs — not section headers,
  not every card border

**Type system (to be chosen explicitly, not by reflex):**
- Display face: warm, institutional, not Fraunces / Crimson / Playfair.
  Candidates worth evaluating: Söhne Breit, GT America Mono, Ligature
  display faces with editorial provenance.
- Body face: refined sans that reads well at body sizes. Not Inter /
  Plus Jakarta / DM Sans. Candidates: Söhne, GT Alpina, Lyon Text,
  Untitled Sans.
- Avoid monospace-as-technical-vibe shorthand. Use monospace only for
  code, IDs, and data that benefits from fixed-width.

### Accessibility

- **Target:** WCAG 2.1 AA for all public pages.
- **Reduced motion:** respect `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere,
  especially in the deck and any hover reveals.
- **Projector visibility:** decks should withstand low-contrast
  conference-room projectors — body text ≥ 28pt equivalent, numbers
  and key phrases heavier-weight, avoid thin strokes.
- **Color-blind:** status chips must carry a label, not color alone
  (already true in the Intervention schema).

## Design Principles

1. **Every claim names a human.** The page that shows what UI is doing
   must show *who is doing it*. Owners are load-bearing UI.
2. **Restraint > decoration.** A single accent gold, asymmetric
   left-aligned layouts, generous white space. No gold stripe on every
   card, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no bouncy motion. If a
   visual element doesn't carry information or weight, cut it.
3. **Density with hierarchy.** Stakeholders want to scan; practitioners
   want detail. Same page can serve both if type hierarchy is steep
   enough (1.25× minimum between steps) and key names can be picked
   out at a glance.
4. **Light surfaces, charcoal ink, gold sparingly.** Tint neutrals
   toward gold subtly for cohesion, but surface dominance is near-white.
   Gold is reserved for emphasis and only emphasis.
5. **The site demonstrates what it argues.** AISPEG preaches
   evidence-forward, owner-named, compliance-aware AI work. The site
   chrome must live up to that: no decorative "trust us" flourishes;
   every design choice should be defensible as functional.
